# Letters for Minerva

**Letters for Minerva** is a lightweight, student-centered web app designed to help prospective university applicants (especially first-generation and international students) evaluate their potential recommendation letter writers.

Choosing who to ask for a recommendation can be one of the most stressful parts of the application process. Letters for Minerva provides a structured way to reflect on these choices and ensures students are prepared when they finally reach out.

## 🚀 Features

- **Guided Evaluation:** A simple 8-question questionnaire that assesses the strength of a potential recommender based on depth, context, and enthusiasm.
- **Supportive Scoring:** Logic-based results categorized as *Strong Choice*, *Could Work*, or *Not the Best Fit*.
- **Actionable Guidance:** Personalized next steps on how to approach the recommender.
- **Preparation Checklist:** A list of essential materials (brag sheets, deadlines, etc.) to share with the recommender.
- **Mobile-Friendly:** Responsive design for students on the go.
- **Privacy-First:** No data collection, no login, and no backend. All logic runs entirely in the browser.

## 🛠️ Built With

- **HTML5 & Vanilla JavaScript** — For a fast, dependency-free experience.
- **CSS Custom Properties** — For clean, modern styling.
- **Google Fonts (Inter)** — For high readability and a friendly tone.

## 📖 How it Works

1. **Home:** Learn about the purpose of the tool and the importance of choosing the right recommender.
2. **Questionnaire:** Answer questions about how well the person knows you, in what context, and their likely supportiveness.
3. **Results:** View your evaluation category and read the "What this means" section.
4. **Prepare:** Follow the "Next Steps" and use the "Checklist" to gather everything you need before asking.

## ⚖️ Disclaimer

Letters for Minerva is a guidance tool intended for reflection. It is not an official admissions tool and does not predict admissions outcomes or guarantee the quality of a recommendation letter.
